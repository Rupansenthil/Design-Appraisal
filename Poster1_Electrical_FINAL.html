05 — Drive System & Gear Ratio
Gear Selection & Torque Validation (F-18FS-2852 Datasheet)
Gear Ratio = Axle ÷ Motor = 60 ÷ 10 = 6:1
Motor max-eff. torque (datasheet) = 1.87 mN·m @ 6700 RPM
Axle torque = 1.87 × 6 = 11.22 mN·m (per motor)
Dual motor axle torque = 11.22 × 2 = 22.44 mN·m total
Axle RPM = 6700 ÷ 6 = 1,117 RPM
Wheel Ø=50mm → v = π×0.05×1117/60 ≈ 2.9 m/s
Stall torque per motor = 11.37 mN·m × 6 = 68.2 mN·m at axle
Motor op. range: 1.5–6V | Nominal: 2.4V | Operating @ 4V → above nominal → higher speed & torque
Why 6:1 & F-18FS: 20m track favours acceleration. 6:1 gives 22.4 mN·m combined axle torque — sufficient to overcome rolling resistance and launch reliably under variable solar conditions. F-18FS chosen over TT motor (lower torque) and N20 (insufficient at 4V) based on datasheet torque values and 1.5–6V operating range matching our 4V array.
06 — Navigation System
Track Guidance

Two Ø6mm ball bearings on vertical poles beneath the chassis ride in the track slot. Passive low-friction guidance through the chicane. Pole spacing: ~95mm centre-to-centre.
