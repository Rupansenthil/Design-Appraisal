03 — Innovative Features (indicated on prototype)
Three Innovative Features
01
Solar Panel Shield
Translucent white-tinted flaps cover panels during rest, protecting from rain and excess heat. Folds into side compartments when deployed. Prevents UV degradation and thermal efficiency loss.
Eng. req: Tinted white to reflect heat, translucent for diffuse charging. Folds flat (<5mm profile). Est. reduces panel surface temp by ~5°C vs exposed.
Aesthetics + Practicality
02
Sun-Pattern 3D Wheels
3D printed wheels with radial "sun" spoke pattern. Creates a dynamic visual spinning effect during racing. Enhances sports car aesthetic while maintaining structural integrity at racing speeds.
Eng. req: PLA, 3mm spoke thickness. Hub bore: exact axle Ø for press-fit. Weight ~12g/wheel. Pattern optimised for visual effect at ~500–1,000 RPM axle speed.
Aesthetics + 3D Fabrication
03
Octagonal Undercuts
Two octagonal cutouts in the baseplate reduce chassis weight and create convective airflow channels to cool the motors and capacitors. Dual function: visual appeal and thermal/weight engineering.
Eng. req: Each cut ~40×40mm. Est. weight saving: ~15g per cut vs solid plate. Airflow channels cool motors by natural convection. Octagon chosen for aesthetic appeal vs plain rectangle.
Aerodynamics + Weight Reduction

06 — Design Assumptions & Clarifications
Key Engineering Assumptions
  • Solar panel tilt assumed 0° (flat) as no optimal angle was specified in the brief. Flat mounting simplifies structure and keeps height within the 70mm constraint.
  • F-18FS-2852 motor selected over alternatives (TT motor — lower torque; N20 — insufficient torque at 4V) based on datasheet torque of 1.87 mN·m at max efficiency and 1.5–6V operating range matching 4V array output.
  • Two 5V/1F capacitors connected in parallel → combined capacitance = 2F at 5V. Provides longer energy storage buffer during low-light conditions.
  • Dual rocker switches used for independent control of each motor circuit, allowing staged testing and safe shutdown.
  • Track length of 20m confirmed with teaching team — this directly informed the 6:1 gear ratio selection, prioritising acceleration over top speed on the short course.
  • Navigation bearing size assumed Ø6mm (standard stock); final size confirmed on physical assembly. Pole spacing ~95mm centre-to-centre.
  • Indoor IV curve benchmarked at ~200 lux (overcast). Outdoor performance expected ~5× higher current output based on measured single-panel MPP data.
